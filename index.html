AI Effectiveness (AIEI)

From API Effectiveness as the Control Plane for AI (Khalsa, Nourian, Crawford, Gray).

AIEI = (wQ x Q) + (wL x L) + (wR x R) + (wC x C_norm)
Q: [1-(Defect_AI/Defect_NonAI)] x Test_Pass_Delta x (1-Rework_AI)
L: 1-(Cycle_AI/Cycle_NonAI)
R: (1-CFR_AI) x SLO x Contract_Catch x Uptime x Adaptability
C: (Labor_Saved-Overhead)/(Token+Infra+Tooling); norm=min(raw/Target,1.0)

What AIEI Scores Mean

0.70 - 1.00: Effective
AI is measurably improving outcomes. Quality, speed, reliability, and cost are all contributing positively. Safe to expand AI adoption. Present results to the board with confidence. Focus shifts to optimization and scaling.
0.40 - 0.69: Mixed
AI is producing some value but specific dimensions need attention. Common pattern: speed improves while quality or reliability degrades. Look at the weakest dimension score - that is where the system is leaking. Do not expand AI to new teams until the weakest dimension is addressed. This is where most enterprises land today.
0.00 - 0.39: Technical Debt
AI is not yet effective as a system. The trust-but-verify tax likely exceeds the speed gains. Pause AI expansion. Invest in measurement infrastructure, governance automation, and contract testing before scaling further. This does not mean AI is bad - it means the surrounding system is not ready.

What Each Dimension Tells You

Q (Quality): Is AI-generated work production-grade? Multiplies defect improvement, test pass rates, and rework rates. If any factor collapses (e.g. 40% rework rate), Q collapses. Below 0.5 = AI is creating technical debt faster than value.
L (Lead Time): Is AI actually faster end-to-end? Measures cycle time delta between AI-assisted and non-AI work. L = 0.40 means 40% faster. L negative means AI is slower (review overhead exceeding generation speed). Watch for L improving while system throughput declines.
R (Reliability): Is the system stable under AI acceleration? Product of five factors - if any one collapses, R collapses. R trending down while L trends up = velocity buying fragility. Postman can measure ~70% of R natively today (SLOs, contract catch, uptime).
C (Cost Efficiency): Is AI worth what it costs, all-in? Includes the trust-but-verify tax (senior engineers reviewing AI output) that most organizations ignore. C < 1.0 raw means AI costs more than it saves. TCO typically exceeds expectations by 40-60%.
